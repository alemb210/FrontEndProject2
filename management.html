Time Management

Managing your time can be difficult but is essential to managing stress. Try to cut out "blank" portions of your day, and instead plan what you will do beforehand, even relaxation time. Also, do your best do rid your surroundings of distractions when studying or working. Instead of studying next to your phone, plan breaks ahead of time. One method that practices this is the "pomodoro" method, where study sessions are consistently broken by short breaks. Managing your time will make you feel more free, efficient, and relaxed.

Breaking Down Tasks

Often in our lives we are stumped by tasks that seem too difficult to approach head-on. This leads to discouragement and sometimes accomplishing nothing. An effective method of dealing with this is to break down tasks. Figure out what you need to do to accomplish a task, breaking it into segments you feel you can tackle. Then, create an order for these tasks. This can be as simple as creating an order for your homework from courses to approach a large workload. This will allow you to work efficiently and with purpose!

Exercise

Mental health is actually connected to our physical body in many ways. If we are in poor physical condition, our mental health will deteroriate alongside it. Sedentary lifestyles are often met with with poor mental health due to the lack of blood flow to the brain. This is easily fixed with semi-regular exercise, especially cardiovascular exercise! Although routine and planned exercise is beneficial, simply performing jumping jacks or jogging can boost one's mental health.
